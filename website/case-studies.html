Real estate · Lagos

How a Lagos real estate firm stopped losing leads on weekends

The challenge

The business was running Facebook and Instagram ads that generated strong lead volume — but enquiries that came in on Friday evenings or over the weekend went into a WhatsApp group that nobody actively monitored. By Monday morning, leads had already called a competitor. There was no pipeline, no assignment system, and no visibility into how many leads were actually being lost.

What changed

Opsra's lead management module was connected to the business's Facebook lead forms and WhatsApp inbox. Every incoming lead was automatically captured, AI-scored, and assigned to a rep with a follow-up task. The owner got a notification when a Hot lead came in — even on a Saturday. The team's activity was logged daily, so the head of sales could see who had followed up and who had not.

The results

Within 30 days of going live, lead response rate increased from 38% to 62%. Weekend lead losses dropped to zero — every enquiry was followed up within 4 hours regardless of when it came in. The head of sales stopped spending Monday mornings reconstructing what had happened the previous week. The owner could see the full pipeline from one screen, live, at any time.

SaaS · Abuja

How an Abuja SaaS company made contractor reviews fair and fast

The challenge

The company had 6 contractors across sales and customer success. KPIs were set in a shared document at the start of each quarter — and then effectively forgotten until review time. When reviews came around, contractors and managers often had different recollections of what was agreed, what was achieved, and what had blocked delivery. Conversations were time-consuming and sometimes adversarial. Two strong contractors had already resigned citing unfair treatment.

What changed

Opsra's contractor KPI tracking module moved targets from the shared document into a live system. Each contractor logged their daily activities — calls made, demos completed, accounts contacted — directly into Opsra. Blockers were flagged immediately with a note. Managers could see progress against targets at any point. Both sides had access to the same data before any review conversation started.

The results

Quarterly review meetings went from averaging 3 hours to under 45 minutes. Disputes dropped significantly — when both sides are looking at the same logged data, there is little room for disagreement about what happened. All 3 at-risk contractors chose to renew their agreements. The operations manager reported that blocker flags had helped her intervene earlier on two significant delivery risks that would previously have only surfaced at review time.

Healthcare · Lagos

How a healthcare platform saved 3 at-risk customers before they churned

The challenge

The platform had a recurring revenue model with monthly and annual subscribers. Customer success was managed informally — there was no system tracking engagement levels, no renewal calendar, and no process for reaching out to accounts that had gone quiet. Churn was only discovered when customers cancelled or stopped paying. By that point, re-engaging them was very difficult.

What changed

Opsra's renewals and retention module began tracking each customer account against engagement signals — login frequency, support ticket volume, outstanding issues. Accounts that showed early warning signs were automatically flagged as At Risk. The head of customer success received weekly alerts for flagged accounts, with AI-drafted win-back messages ready to personalise and send. A renewal calendar was set up to trigger outreach 6 weeks before each renewal date.

The results

Within the first 8 weeks, Opsra flagged 3 accounts as At Risk. In all 3 cases, the team had not noticed any issues — the accounts had been quietly disengaging. Personalised outreach was sent within 24 hours of each flag. All 3 accounts were retained after brief conversations that addressed their underlying concerns. The head of customer success noted that without early warning, they would not have reached out until renewal time — at which point 2 of the 3 accounts said they would have cancelled.
