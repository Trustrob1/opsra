About Opsra

We built Opsra because we watched good businesses lose money to bad systems

Most growing businesses in Nigeria are run by smart, capable people — who spend too much of their time chasing updates, managing spreadsheets, and dealing with problems they didn't see coming.

We built Opsra to fix that. A business owner should be able to see everything that is happening in their business from one screen, every morning — and spend the rest of the day actually building the business, not administering it.

Our mission

Give every growing business the operational clarity that used to belong only to large enterprises with dedicated ops teams and expensive software.

How we work

What we believe about building businesses

These are the principles behind how Opsra is built — and why it works the way it does.

Visibility before decisions
You cannot make good decisions about things you cannot see. Every feature we build starts with the question: what does the business owner need to see right now?
Clarity creates fairness
When both sides of a relationship — employer and contractor, manager and team — see the same data, conversations become factual instead of emotional. We build for that.
Simple enough to actually use
A system nobody uses is not a system — it is a subscription. We design Opsra for people who are not technical, who are busy, and who need it to work from day one without a manual.
Early warnings save money
Every churn, every performance problem, and every missed lead shows signs before it becomes expensive. We build Opsra to surface those signs weeks earlier than you would otherwise notice them.
Honest about what works
We tell every prospective client honestly whether Opsra is the right fit for them. A customer who is not ready for a system like this will not get value — and that is not good for either of us.
Built for Nigerian businesses
Opsra is designed for how businesses actually run in Nigeria — WhatsApp-first customer relationships, lean teams, contractor-heavy operations, and owners who wear many hats at once.
